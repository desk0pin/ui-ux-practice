Bhagat Singh

Indian Revolutionary Freedom Fighter

Early Life

Bhagat Singh was born in 1907 in Punjab, India. He became involved in the Indian independence movement at a young age. He was greatly influenced by his family and local leaders who opposed British rule.

Revolutionary Activities

He was a vocal critic of British rule and participated in high-profile attacks on British authorities. His courage and dedication made him a symbol of youth rebellion and a hero of the Indian independence movement.

Legacy

Bhagat Singh was executed in 1931 at the age of 23. Today, he is remembered as a national hero and continues to inspire generations of Indians.
